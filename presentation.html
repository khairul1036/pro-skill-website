Rules & Marking

All participants must adhere to the following rules:

  1. Teams will be evaluated based on set criteria, and each presentation will be scored accordingly.
  2. All participants must be formally dressed. Teams must appear in professional attire.
  3. Teams must include a slide addressing team member contributions, with the slide number specified.
  4. Participants must form teams consisting of 3-4 members.
  5. Cross-department collaboration is allowed.
  6. All members of the team must complete the registration process. If any team member has not completed registration, the team cannot participate in the presentation.
  7. Each team will have 6 minutes to present, followed by a 5-minute Q&A session (as determined by the judges).
  8. Exceeding the 6-minute presentation limit will result in a 10-point deduction per minute.
  9. Presentations must be relevant to the given theme or topic, which will be provided by the judging and problem-setting committee.
  10. Teams should use MS PowerPoint or Google Slides as their presentation medium.
  11. Visual aids, such as images, videos, and graphs, are encouraged but must be relevant and professional.
  12. Teams must test their equipment 15 minutes before the competition to avoid technical issues.
  13. Participants are responsible for bringing their own laptops or devices. Projectors, screens, and sound systems will be provided.
  14. Participants must respect time limits, other teams, and the judges.
  15. No offensive or inappropriate content will be tolerated. Teams that violate this rule will be disqualified.
  16. Late arrivals may not be allowed to present.
  17. Teams must submit their final slides 24 hours before the event for technical checks.
  18. No major changes to the presentation will be allowed after submission.
  19. Any external resources (statistics, quotes, etc.) used must be properly credited.
  20. Internet access will not be available during the competition, so participants must ensure all necessary resources are downloaded beforehand.
  21. If any team creates any issue or does not maintain the rules, the event committee has the authority to disqualify the team.

NB: Marking criteria will be based on performance, creativity, and adherence to the rules. Judges' decisions are final.

What is "Presentation Master" ?

Presentation Master is a competitive contest organized by Empower NextGen Ltd, focused on showcasing and refining participants' presentation skills. The contest features Two Rounds: a Preliminary Round and a Final Round, where each participant is assigned a specific topic by the judges. Competitors must create and submit their presentations 24 hours before the event to ensure timely review. Once submitted, presentations cannot be edited or resubmitted. With a focus on structure, creativity, and clear communication, Presentation Master offers participants the chance to demonstrate their expertise and earn recognition for their presentation skills in a dynamic, supportive environment.
